Common Diseases

Common Cold

A viral infection of your nose and throat (upper respiratory tract).

Causes
  • Viruses, most commonly rhinoviruses.
Symptoms
  • Runny or stuffy nose
  • Sore throat
  • Cough
  • Congestion
Prevention
  • Wash hands frequently
  • Avoid touching face
  • Avoid close contact with sick people

Influenza (Flu)

A contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses.

Causes
  • Influenza viruses.
Symptoms
  • Fever or feeling feverish/chills
  • Cough
  • Sore throat
  • Muscle or body aches
Prevention
  • Get vaccinated annually
  • Avoid close contact with sick people
  • Wash hands often

Malaria

A serious and sometimes fatal disease caused by a parasite that commonly infects a certain type of mosquito which feeds on humans.

Causes
  • Plasmodium parasites, transmitted by Anopheles mosquitoes.
Symptoms
  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Headache
  • Nausea and vomiting
Prevention
  • Use mosquito nets
  • Wear protective clothing
  • Use insect repellent
  • Take antimalarial drugs
Treatment
  • Antimalarial medications.

Dengue Fever

A mosquito-borne tropical disease caused by the dengue virus.

Causes
  • Dengue virus, transmitted by Aedes mosquitoes.
Symptoms
  • High fever
  • Severe headache
  • Pain behind the eyes
  • Joint and muscle pain
Prevention
  • Eliminate mosquito breeding sites
  • Use insect repellent
  • Wear protective clothing
Treatment
  • Supportive care, pain relievers.

Typhoid

A bacterial infection that can lead to a high fever, diarrhea, and vomiting.

Causes
  • Salmonella Typhi bacteria, spread through contaminated food or water.
Symptoms
  • High fever
  • Weakness
  • Stomach pain
  • Headache
Prevention
  • Get vaccinated
  • Wash hands thoroughly
  • Drink safe water
  • Eat safely prepared food
Treatment
  • Antibiotics.

Tuberculosis (TB)

A potentially serious infectious disease that mainly affects your lungs.

Causes
  • Mycobacterium tuberculosis bacteria, spread through the air.
Symptoms
  • Persistent cough
  • Chest pain
  • Weight loss
  • Fever and night sweats
Prevention
  • BCG vaccine
  • Avoid close contact with infected individuals
  • Good ventilation
Treatment
  • Long course of antibiotics.

Diabetes

A chronic disease that occurs either when the pancreas does not produce enough insulin or when the body cannot effectively use the insulin it produces.

Causes
  • Genetics, obesity, lack of physical activity.
Symptoms
  • Increased thirst and urination
  • Increased hunger
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue
Prevention
  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Regular exercise
  • Balanced diet
Treatment
  • Insulin, oral medications, diet, exercise.

Hypertension

Also known as high blood pressure, it is a common condition in which the long-term force of the blood against your artery walls is high enough that it may eventually cause health problems.

Causes
  • Genetics, unhealthy diet, lack of exercise, obesity, stress.
Symptoms
  • Often no symptoms (silent killer)
  • Headaches
  • Shortness of breath
  • Nosebleeds (in severe cases)
Prevention
  • Healthy diet (low sodium)
  • Regular exercise
  • Maintain healthy weight
  • Limit alcohol
Treatment
  • Lifestyle changes, medications (e.g., diuretics, ACE inhibitors).

Asthma

A condition in which your airways narrow and swell and may produce extra mucus.

Causes
  • Genetics, environmental factors, allergens, respiratory infections.
Symptoms
  • Shortness of breath
  • Chest tightness or pain
  • Wheezing
  • Coughing
Prevention
  • Avoid triggers
  • Follow asthma action plan
  • Get flu vaccine
Treatment
  • Inhalers (bronchodilators, corticosteroids), oral medications.

Pneumonia

An infection that inflames air sacs in one or both lungs, which may fill with fluid or pus.

Causes
  • Bacteria, viruses, fungi.
Symptoms
  • Cough with phlegm
  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Shortness of breath
Prevention
  • Get vaccinated (pneumococcal, flu)
  • Wash hands
  • Don't smoke
Treatment
  • Antibiotics (bacterial), antiviral drugs (viral), supportive care.

COVID-19

An infectious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus.

Causes
  • SARS-CoV-2 virus, spread through respiratory droplets.
Symptoms
  • Fever or chills
  • Cough
  • Fatigue
  • Loss of taste or smell
Prevention
  • Vaccination
  • Wear masks
  • Social distancing
  • Hand hygiene
Treatment
  • Supportive care, antiviral medications (in some cases).

Chickenpox

A highly contagious infection caused by the varicella-zoster virus.

Causes
  • Varicella-zoster virus, spread through direct contact or airborne droplets.
Symptoms
  • Itchy, blister-like rash
  • Fever
  • Fatigue
  • Loss of appetite
Prevention
  • Chickenpox vaccine
  • Avoid contact with infected individuals
Treatment
  • Supportive care, antiviral drugs (in some cases).

Measles

A highly contagious infectious disease caused by a virus.

Causes
  • Measles virus, spread through respiratory droplets.
Symptoms
  • High fever
  • Cough
  • Runny nose
  • Red, watery eyes
  • Rash
Prevention
  • MMR vaccine
  • Avoid contact with infected individuals
Treatment
  • Supportive care.

Hepatitis (Jaundice)

Inflammation of the liver, often causing yellowing of the skin and eyes (jaundice).

Causes
  • Viral infections (Hepatitis A, B, C, D, E), alcohol, certain drugs.
Symptoms
  • Yellow skin and eyes
  • Fatigue
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Dark urine
Prevention
  • Vaccination (Hepatitis A, B)
  • Safe sex practices
  • Avoid sharing needles
  • Limit alcohol intake
Treatment
  • Antiviral medications (for chronic viral hepatitis), supportive care.

Cholera

An acute diarrheal illness caused by infection of the intestine with Vibrio cholerae bacteria.

Causes
  • Vibrio cholerae bacteria, spread through contaminated water or food.
Symptoms
  • Severe watery diarrhea
  • Vomiting
  • Leg cramps
  • Dehydration
Prevention
  • Drink safe water
  • Wash hands with soap and water
  • Cook food thoroughly
  • Oral cholera vaccine
Treatment
  • Rehydration therapy, antibiotics.

Migraine

A type of headache that can cause severe throbbing pain or a pulsing sensation, usually on one side of the head.

Causes
  • Genetics, environmental factors, certain triggers (stress, foods, hormones).
Symptoms
  • Severe headache
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Sensitivity to light and sound
  • Aura (visual disturbances)
Prevention
  • Identify and avoid triggers
  • Stress management
  • Regular sleep schedule
Treatment
  • Pain relievers, triptans, CGRP inhibitors.

Arthritis

Inflammation of one or more joints, causing pain and stiffness.

Causes
  • Autoimmune disorders, wear and tear, infections, injuries.
Symptoms
  • Joint pain
  • Stiffness
  • Swelling
  • Reduced range of motion
Prevention
  • Maintain healthy weight
  • Regular exercise
  • Protect joints from injury
Treatment
  • Medications (pain relievers, anti-inflammatories), physical therapy, surgery.

Heart Disease

A range of conditions that affect your heart, including coronary artery disease, heart attack, and heart failure.

Causes
  • High blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes, smoking, obesity, genetics.
Symptoms
  • Chest pain (angina)
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue
  • Swelling in legs, ankles, feet
Prevention
  • Healthy diet
  • Regular exercise
  • Maintain healthy weight
  • Manage stress
  • Quit smoking
Treatment
  • Lifestyle changes, medications (e.g., statins, beta-blockers), surgery.

Obesity

A complex disease involving an excessive amount of body fat.

Causes
  • Genetics, unhealthy diet, lack of physical activity, certain medications.
Symptoms
  • Excess body fat
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue
  • Joint pain
Prevention
  • Balanced diet
  • Regular exercise
  • Portion control
Treatment
  • Diet and exercise, medications, bariatric surgery.

Anemia

A condition in which you lack enough healthy red blood cells to carry adequate oxygen to your body's tissues.

Causes
  • Iron deficiency, vitamin deficiencies, chronic diseases, blood loss.
Symptoms
  • Fatigue
  • Weakness
  • Pale skin
  • Shortness of breath
Prevention
  • Diet rich in iron and vitamins
  • Treat underlying conditions
Treatment
  • Iron supplements, vitamin supplements, treating underlying cause.

Cancer

A disease caused by an uncontrolled division of abnormal cells in a part of the body.

Causes
  • Genetics, environmental factors (e.g., smoking, UV radiation), viruses.
Symptoms
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue
  • Pain
  • Changes in skin or bowel habits
Prevention
  • Healthy lifestyle
  • Avoid tobacco
  • Sun protection
  • Regular screenings
Treatment
  • Surgery, chemotherapy, radiation therapy, immunotherapy.

Kidney Disease

A condition in which your kidneys are damaged and can't filter blood as well as they should.

Causes
  • Diabetes, high blood pressure, autoimmune diseases, infections.
Symptoms
  • Fatigue
  • Swelling in ankles and feet
  • Muscle cramps
  • Changes in urination
Prevention
  • Manage diabetes and high blood pressure
  • Healthy diet
  • Regular exercise
Treatment
  • Medications, dialysis, kidney transplant.

Skin Allergy

An immune system reaction to a substance that is usually harmless.

Causes
  • Allergens (e.g., pollen, dust mites, certain foods, chemicals).
Symptoms
  • Redness
  • Itching
  • Rash
  • Swelling
Prevention
  • Identify and avoid allergens
  • Use hypoallergenic products
Treatment
  • Antihistamines, corticosteroids, moisturizers.

Food Poisoning

Illness caused by eating contaminated food.

Causes
  • Bacteria, viruses, parasites, toxins in food.
Symptoms
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Diarrhea
  • Abdominal cramps
Prevention
  • Cook food thoroughly
  • Wash hands and surfaces
  • Refrigerate food promptly
Treatment
  • Rehydration, antibiotics (bacterial), supportive care.

Stroke

Occurs when the blood supply to part of your brain is interrupted or reduced, depriving brain tissue of oxygen and nutrients.

Causes
  • High blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes, smoking, heart disease.
Symptoms
  • Sudden weakness or numbness on one side of the body
  • Difficulty speaking or understanding speech
  • Vision problems
  • Severe headache
Prevention
  • Manage blood pressure and cholesterol
  • Healthy diet
  • Regular exercise
  • Quit smoking
Treatment
  • Emergency medications (e.g., tPA), surgery, rehabilitation.

Depression

A mood disorder that causes a persistent feeling of sadness and loss of interest.

Causes
  • Genetics, brain chemistry, stress, trauma.
Symptoms
  • Persistent sadness
  • Loss of interest in activities
  • Changes in appetite or sleep
  • Fatigue
Prevention
  • Stress management
  • Healthy lifestyle
  • Seek support
Treatment
  • Psychotherapy, antidepressant medications.

Anxiety Disorder

A type of mental health condition characterized by feelings of worry, nervousness, or fear that are strong enough to interfere with daily activities.

Causes
  • Genetics, brain chemistry, personality, life events.
Symptoms
  • Excessive worry
  • Restlessness
  • Fatigue
  • Difficulty concentrating
Prevention
  • Stress management techniques
  • Regular exercise
  • Avoid caffeine and alcohol
Treatment
  • Psychotherapy, anti-anxiety medications.

HIV/AIDS

HIV (human immunodeficiency virus) is a virus that attacks the body's immune system. If not treated, it can lead to AIDS (acquired immunodeficiency syndrome).

Causes
  • HIV virus, transmitted through certain body fluids (blood, semen, vaginal fluids, breast milk).
Symptoms
  • Flu-like symptoms (early stage)
  • Weight loss
  • Fever
  • Night sweats
  • Opportunistic infections (later stages)
Prevention
  • Safe sex practices
  • Avoid sharing needles
  • PrEP (pre-exposure prophylaxis)
Treatment
  • Antiretroviral therapy (ART).

Hepatitis B

A serious liver infection caused by the hepatitis B virus (HBV).

Causes
  • Hepatitis B virus, transmitted through blood, semen, or other body fluids.
Symptoms
  • Fatigue
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Abdominal pain
  • Jaundice
Prevention
  • Hepatitis B vaccine
  • Safe sex practices
  • Avoid sharing needles
Treatment
  • Antiviral medications (for chronic infection), supportive care.

Hepatitis C

A viral infection that causes liver inflammation, sometimes leading to serious liver damage.

Causes
  • Hepatitis C virus, transmitted through contact with infected blood.
Symptoms
  • Often asymptomatic until liver damage occurs
  • Fatigue
  • Nausea
  • Dark urine
  • Jaundice
Prevention
  • Avoid sharing needles
  • Safe injection practices
  • No vaccine available
Treatment
  • Antiviral medications.

Peptic Ulcer

Sores that develop on the lining of the esophagus, stomach, or small intestine.

Causes
  • H. pylori infection, long-term use of NSAIDs (nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs).
Symptoms
  • Burning stomach pain
  • Bloating
  • Heartburn
  • Nausea
Prevention
  • Avoid NSAID overuse
  • Manage stress
  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol
Treatment
  • Antibiotics (for H. pylori), proton pump inhibitors, antacids.

Sinusitis

Inflammation of the tissue lining the sinuses.

Causes
  • Viruses, bacteria, fungi, allergies.
Symptoms
  • Facial pain or pressure
  • Stuffy nose
  • Headache
  • Cough
Prevention
  • Avoid allergens
  • Wash hands
  • Avoid smoking
Treatment
  • Decongestants, nasal corticosteroids, antibiotics (bacterial).

Bronchitis

Inflammation of the lining of your bronchial tubes, which carry air to and from your lungs.

Causes
  • Viruses (acute), smoking, air pollution (chronic).
Symptoms
  • Cough with mucus
  • Fatigue
  • Shortness of breath
  • Chest discomfort
Prevention
  • Avoid smoking
  • Wash hands
  • Get flu vaccine
Treatment
  • Rest, fluids, bronchodilators, antibiotics (bacterial).

Tonsillitis

Inflammation of the tonsils, two oval-shaped pads of tissue at the back of your throat.

Causes
  • Viral or bacterial infection (e.g., strep throat).
Symptoms
  • Sore throat
  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Fever
  • Swollen tonsils
Prevention
  • Wash hands frequently
  • Avoid sharing food and drinks
Treatment
  • Antibiotics (bacterial), pain relievers, tonsillectomy (recurrent cases).

Cataract

A clouding of the normally clear lens of your eye.

Causes
  • Aging, diabetes, eye injury, prolonged steroid use.
Symptoms
  • Cloudy or blurry vision
  • Difficulty with night vision
  • Sensitivity to light
  • Fading or yellowing of colors
Prevention
  • Protect eyes from UV light
  • Manage diabetes
  • Healthy diet
Treatment
  • Surgery to replace the cloudy lens.

Glaucoma

A group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, often caused by abnormally high pressure in your eye.

Causes
  • Increased intraocular pressure, genetics, age.
Symptoms
  • Often no early symptoms
  • Gradual loss of peripheral vision
  • Eye pain (acute angle-closure glaucoma)
Prevention
  • Regular eye exams
  • Manage underlying health conditions
Treatment
  • Eye drops, oral medications, laser treatment, surgery.

Malnutrition

A condition that results from eating a diet in which nutrients are either not enough or are too much such that the diet causes health problems.

Causes
  • Insufficient food intake, poor diet quality, underlying diseases affecting nutrient absorption.
Symptoms
  • Weight loss
  • Fatigue
  • Weakened immune system
  • Stunted growth (children)
Prevention
  • Balanced diet
  • Access to nutritious food
  • Address underlying health issues
Treatment
  • Nutritional support, dietary changes, treating underlying causes.

Appendicitis

An inflammation of the appendix, a finger-shaped pouch that projects from your colon on the lower right side of your abdomen.

Causes
  • Blockage in the lining of the appendix, often by stool, foreign body, or infection.
Symptoms
  • Sudden pain that begins around your navel and shifts to your lower right abdomen
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Loss of appetite
  • Fever
Prevention
  • No specific prevention, but a high-fiber diet may reduce risk.
Treatment
  • Surgery to remove the appendix (appendectomy).

Epilepsy

A central nervous system (neurological) disorder in which brain activity becomes abnormal, causing seizures or periods of unusual behavior, sensations, and sometimes loss of awareness.

Causes
  • Genetics, brain injury, stroke, infections, developmental disorders.
Symptoms
  • Temporary confusion
  • A staring spell
  • Uncontrollable jerking movements of the arms and legs
  • Loss of consciousness or awareness
Prevention
  • Manage underlying conditions
  • Avoid head injuries
Treatment
  • Anti-seizure medications, surgery, vagus nerve stimulation.

Psoriasis

A chronic autoimmune condition that causes the rapid buildup of skin cells, resulting in thick, silvery scales and itchy, dry, red patches.

Causes
  • Autoimmune reaction, genetics, triggers (stress, infections, skin injury).
Symptoms
  • Red patches of skin covered with thick, silvery scales
  • Dry, cracked skin that may bleed
  • Itching, burning, or soreness
  • Thickened, pitted, or ridged nails
Prevention
  • Avoid triggers
  • Moisturize skin
  • Stress management
Treatment
  • Topical creams, phototherapy, oral medications, biologics.

Eczema

A condition that causes your skin to become dry, itchy, and inflamed.

Causes
  • Genetics, immune system dysfunction, environmental triggers (allergens, irritants).
Symptoms
  • Dry, itchy skin
  • Red or brownish-gray patches
  • Small, raised bumps
  • Cracked, scaly skin
Prevention
  • Moisturize regularly
  • Avoid triggers
  • Use mild soaps
Treatment
  • Topical corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, antihistamines, phototherapy.

Gout

A form of arthritis characterized by severe pain, redness, and tenderness in joints.

Causes
  • High levels of uric acid in the blood, diet rich in purines, alcohol consumption.
Symptoms
  • Sudden, severe joint pain (often in the big toe)
  • Swelling
  • Redness
  • Tenderness
Prevention
  • Limit purine-rich foods
  • Avoid alcohol
  • Stay hydrated
  • Maintain healthy weight
Treatment
  • NSAIDs, colchicine, corticosteroids (for acute attacks), allopurinol (for prevention).

Rabies

A deadly virus spread to people from the saliva of infected animals.

Causes
  • Rabies virus, transmitted through bites or scratches from infected animals.
Symptoms
  • Fever
  • Headache
  • Nausea
  • Anxiety
  • Hydrophobia (fear of water)
Prevention
  • Vaccinate pets
  • Avoid contact with wild animals
  • Pre-exposure vaccination for high-risk individuals
Treatment
  • Post-exposure prophylaxis (vaccine and immunoglobulin) immediately after exposure.

Tetanus

A serious bacterial infection that causes painful muscle spasms and can lead to death.

Causes
  • Clostridium tetani bacteria, found in soil and animal feces, enters the body through wounds.
Symptoms
  • Muscle spasms (especially in jaw and neck)
  • Stiffness
  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Fever
Prevention
  • Tetanus vaccine (DTP, Tdap, Td)
  • Clean wounds thoroughly
Treatment
  • Tetanus antitoxin, antibiotics, muscle relaxants, supportive care.

Polio

A disabling and life-threatening disease caused by the poliovirus.

Causes
  • Poliovirus, spread through contaminated food or water, or contact with an infected person.
Symptoms
  • Most cases are asymptomatic
  • Fever
  • Fatigue
  • Headache
  • Muscle weakness and paralysis (severe cases)
Prevention
  • Polio vaccine (IPV or OPV)
Treatment
  • Supportive care, physical therapy for paralysis.

Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)

An infection in any part of your urinary system — your kidneys, ureters, bladder and urethra.

Causes
  • Bacteria (most commonly E. coli) entering the urinary tract.
Symptoms
  • Strong, persistent urge to urinate
  • Burning sensation when urinating
  • Frequent, small amounts of urine
  • Cloudy, strong-smelling urine
Prevention
  • Drink plenty of water
  • Wipe front to back
  • Urinate after intercourse
  • Avoid irritating feminine products
Treatment
  • Antibiotics.

Osteoporosis

A condition that causes bones to become weak and brittle — so brittle that a fall or even mild stresses like coughing can cause a fracture.

Causes
  • Aging, hormonal changes (menopause), calcium and vitamin D deficiency, certain medications.
Symptoms
  • Often no symptoms until a fracture occurs
  • Back pain (from fractured vertebra)
  • Loss of height over time
  • Stooped posture
Prevention
  • Adequate calcium and vitamin D intake
  • Weight-bearing exercise
  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol
Treatment
  • Bisphosphonates, hormone therapy, lifestyle changes.

Insomnia

A common sleep disorder that can make it hard to fall asleep, hard to stay asleep, or cause you to wake up too early and not be able to get back to sleep.

Causes
  • Stress, anxiety, depression, poor sleep habits, certain medications, medical conditions.
Symptoms
  • Difficulty falling asleep
  • Waking up during the night
  • Waking up too early
  • Daytime fatigue
Prevention
  • Maintain a regular sleep schedule
  • Create a relaxing bedtime routine
  • Avoid caffeine and alcohol before bed
  • Regular exercise
Treatment
  • Cognitive behavioral therapy for insomnia (CBT-I), sleep medications (short-term).

Hypothyroidism

A condition in which your thyroid gland doesn't produce enough of certain crucial hormones.

Causes
  • Autoimmune disease (Hashimoto's), thyroid surgery, radiation therapy, certain medications.
Symptoms
  • Fatigue
  • Weight gain
  • Constipation
  • Dry skin
  • Sensitivity to cold
Prevention
  • No specific prevention, but regular screening for high-risk individuals.
Treatment
  • Thyroid hormone replacement medication (levothyroxine).

Hyperthyroidism

A condition in which your thyroid gland produces too much of the hormone thyroxine.

Causes
  • Graves' disease, thyroid nodules, thyroiditis.
Symptoms
  • Weight loss
  • Rapid or irregular heartbeat
  • Nervousness, anxiety, irritability
  • Tremor in hands or fingers
  • Increased sensitivity to heat
Prevention
  • No specific prevention.
Treatment
  • Antithyroid medications, radioactive iodine therapy, surgery.

Eye Infection

An infection that can affect any part of the eye, including the eyelid, conjunctiva, cornea, or inner eye structures.

Causes
  • Bacteria, viruses, fungi, parasites.
Symptoms
  • Redness
  • Itching
  • Discharge
  • Pain
  • Blurred vision
Prevention
  • Wash hands frequently
  • Avoid touching eyes
  • Don't share eye makeup
  • Proper contact lens hygiene
Treatment
  • Antibiotic eye drops (bacterial), antiviral eye drops (viral), antifungal medications.
